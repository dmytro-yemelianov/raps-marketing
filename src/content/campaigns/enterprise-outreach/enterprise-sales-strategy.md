# Enterprise Sales Strategy: "Production-Ready APS Automation"

> **Note**: This is an aspirational planning document. RAPS is currently an open-source project (Apache-2.0) by a solo developer. Enterprise features like SSO, SLA guarantees, and pricing tiers described below are future goals, not current offerings. Customer stories and company names are hypothetical examples.

## Strategic Overview

**Objective**: Develop enterprise adoption strategy for RAPS CLI automation solutions.

**Core Value Proposition**: *"Transform from manual APS operations to enterprise-grade automated workflows with zero-downtime deployment and comprehensive compliance."*

## Sales Process & Methodology

### 📋 **Enterprise Sales Funnel**

#### **Stage 1: Awareness & Interest (Months 1-2)**
**Objective**: Identify prospects and establish initial contact

**Activities:**
1. **Content Marketing**: Thought leadership articles in trade publications
2. **Conference Speaking**: Present at major industry events (AU, AIA, etc.)
3. **Webinar Series**: "Enterprise APS Automation" technical webinars
4. **LinkedIn Outreach**: Targeted connection requests to decision makers
5. **Partner Referrals**: Leverage Autodesk Partner network for introductions

**Success Metrics:**
- 100+ qualified enterprise leads identified
- 50+ meaningful initial conversations
- 25+ webinar attendees requesting follow-up meetings

#### **Stage 2: Qualification & Discovery (Months 2-3)**
**Objective**: Understand enterprise requirements and pain points

**Discovery Questions Framework:**
1. **Current State Assessment**:
   - "How many models does your team process monthly?"
   - "What's your current approach to APS automation?"
   - "Where do bottlenecks occur in your workflow?"
   - "What compliance requirements must you meet?"

2. **Pain Point Analysis**:
   - "How much time does your team spend on manual APS operations?"
   - "What happens when translation jobs fail over weekends?"
   - "How do you ensure quality control across projects?"
   - "What are your biggest concerns about scaling current processes?"

3. **Decision Making Process**:
   - "Who else would be involved in evaluating automation solutions?"
   - "What criteria will you use to make this decision?"
   - "What's your timeline for implementing a solution?"
   - "What budget range are you considering?"

**Qualification Criteria:**
- ✅ 500+ employees or $50M+ annual revenue
- ✅ High-volume APS usage (100+ operations/month)
- ✅ Identified pain points that RAPS directly addresses
- ✅ Budget authority and decision-making timeline
- ✅ Technical team capable of automation implementation

#### **Stage 3: Solution Design & Proposal (Months 3-4)**
**Objective**: Create customized enterprise solution addressing specific needs

**Proposal Components:**
1. **Technical Architecture**:
   - Customized deployment architecture for their environment
   - Integration points with existing systems
   - Security and compliance implementation plan
   - Performance requirements and capacity planning

2. **Implementation Plan**:
   - Phased rollout approach (pilot → department → enterprise)
   - Training and change management strategy
   - Success metrics and monitoring approach
   - Risk mitigation and contingency planning

3. **Commercial Structure**:
   - Software licensing (open source + commercial features)
   - Professional services for implementation and training
   - Ongoing support and maintenance agreements
   - Success-based pricing options where appropriate

#### **Stage 4: Pilot Program (Months 4-5)**
**Objective**: Demonstrate value through limited production deployment

**Pilot Program Structure:**
1. **Scope Definition**:
   - Single department or project team (10-25 users)
   - 2-3 core workflows representing broader use cases
   - 60-90 day evaluation period with clear success metrics
   - Regular check-ins and feedback collection

2. **Success Metrics**:
   - 50%+ reduction in manual processing time
   - 90%+ reliability in automated operations
   - Positive user feedback and adoption rates
   - Clear path to enterprise-wide deployment

3. **Support Structure**:
   - Dedicated implementation engineer
   - Weekly check-ins and troubleshooting support
   - Direct access to development team for feature requests
   - Comprehensive training for internal team

#### **Stage 5: Enterprise Contract (Months 5-6)**
**Objective**: Secure enterprise-wide deployment contract

**Contract Components:**
1. **Software Licensing**:
   - Enterprise license for unlimited users
   - Access to commercial features (enterprise auth, advanced monitoring)
   - Source code escrow for risk mitigation
   - Future feature development roadmap input

2. **Professional Services**:
   - Implementation consulting and architecture review
   - Custom integration development for existing systems
   - Training programs for technical and business users
   - Change management support for organization adoption

3. **Support & Maintenance**:
   - 24/7 enterprise support with guaranteed response times
   - Regular health checks and optimization reviews
   - Priority bug fixes and feature development
   - Annual strategic planning sessions
