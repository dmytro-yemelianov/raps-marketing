## Rate Justification Points

When clients question rates, reference:

1. **Specialized Expertise**
   - "APS expertise is rare - there are fewer than 500 active Forge/APS developers globally"
   - "My solutions are validated against official Autodesk OpenAPI specifications"

2. **Production Quality**
   - "Zero-runtime-dependency binaries that work across all platforms"
   - "Enterprise-grade code with comprehensive error handling"

3. **Open Source Credibility**
   - "Creator of RAPS, the most comprehensive open-source APS CLI"
   - "Active GitHub contributor with production-tested code"

4. **Cost Savings**
   - "Automation typically saves 10-50 hours/week of manual work"
   - "Preventing API mistakes saves thousands in debugging"

## Discounting Strategy

### When to Offer Discounts

| Scenario | Discount | Conditions |
|----------|----------|------------|
| First Project | 10-15% | For building relationship/reviews |
| Long-term Contract (3+ months) | 15-20% | Guaranteed hours |
| Retainer Agreement | 20-25% | Monthly minimum commitment |
| Open Source / Nonprofit | 25-40% | Case-by-case |
| Testimonial/Case Study Rights | 10% | Written approval for marketing |

### When NOT to Discount

- Rush/urgent projects (charge premium instead)
- Scope creep situations
- Clients who negotiate excessively
- Projects with unclear requirements

## Payment Terms

### Standard Terms
- 50% upfront, 50% on completion (projects < $10K)
- 40/30/30 split for larger projects (milestone-based)
- Net 14 for enterprise clients with PO

### Upwork-Specific
- Follow Upwork payment protection
- Use milestones for fixed-price
- Weekly billing for hourly contracts

## Rate Increases

- Increase rates 10-15% annually
- Announce to existing clients 60 days ahead
- Grandfather rates for active projects

### Trigger Points for Rate Increases
- After 10+ successful Upwork projects
- Top Rated / Expert-Vetted status
- Major RAPS release or milestone
